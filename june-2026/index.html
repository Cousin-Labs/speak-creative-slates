June creative bets — goal: higher P2 win rate via install-to-trial

Expanding the foundation of high-conviction iterative work while pushing more derivative concepts to build format diversity in channel. ITR is the goal KPI — the bets below are structured to create more at-bats at P2 with higher starting confidence.

  • Embed (P2) format dominates the slate — majority of iterative concepts run social proof or reaction moments before product reveal, the pattern most correlated with high ITR in existing BAU winners
  • Iterating on proven P2 structures rather than testing new formats from scratch — Salon Surprise, She Was Shook, Telenovela, Taco Bell all have ITR data to beat; v2.0 tightens the hook and proof sequence
  • Derivative expansion to isolate format vs. creator — taking formats with one proven winner (Kitchen POV, No-Sabo Classroom) into new scenarios to confirm format is the variable, not the moment
  • New creator casting into proven slots — Catherine and Gavriel slotted into established concept structures so the only variable is creator fit
  • Recuts as low-cost P2 at-bats — remixing top BAU assets to test whether combined proof moments outperform individual cuts, no net-new production spend
  • Expanding into P1 Direct as a parallel track — Speak is intentionally testing more product messaging alongside scenario work; functional hooks, direct outcome framing, and wider entry points (Before & After, expert voice) are the early bets in that direction
